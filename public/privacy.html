2. Data we collect

2.1 Data you provide directly

When you use the Service, you send us the information required to build your resume. This may include:

  • Your full name
  • Personal email address
  • LinkedIn profile URL
  • GitHub profile URL and public repository URLs
  • Coding-profile URLs (LeetCode, Codeforces, HackerRank, etc.) and stated statistics
  • Educational details — degree, institution, branch of study, expected year of graduation, CGPA (optional)
  • Coursework subjects
  • Technical skills
  • Work experience — role, company, dates, responsibilities, achievements
  • Projects — name, technology stack, description, outcomes, links
  • Positions of responsibility, certifications, awards and achievements
  • Target job description text or role name for tailoring the resume
  • Any free-text edits or clarifications you send while iterating on your resume
  • Support correspondence (email content, screenshots you share)

2.2 Data collected automatically

  • Your WhatsApp phone number — required for WhatsApp to deliver our reply. See §2.3 on how we store this.
  • Message timestamps and identifiers — required for deduplicating retried webhooks.
  • Payment metadata — a payment identifier, amount, and timestamp for each ₹49 unlock. We do not receive or store your UPI ID, card number, CVV, or bank credentials — these are handled entirely by our payment gateway (Razorpay Software Private Limited).
  • Diagnostic logs — anonymized system events used to monitor uptime, latency, and errors. Phone numbers are hashed before being written to logs.

2.3 How we store your phone number

Your raw phone number is never written to our logs, our database, or any third-party analytics tool. Before persistence, we compute a peppered SHA-256 HMAC hash of your number (using a secret key held only by our production server) and use only that hash as your session identifier. The raw phone number lives briefly in encrypted session storage so we can send our reply back to you, and is discarded after your session expires.

2.4 Data from third parties

  • GitHub, Inc. — when you send us a public GitHub repository URL, we call GitHub's public API to fetch repository metadata and a README excerpt (up to 2 500 characters) to help our AI describe your project accurately. We only fetch information that is already publicly visible to anyone.
  • Naukri.com (Info Edge (India) Ltd.) — when you send us a public Naukri job URL, we fetch the public job-description page for tailoring. We never send your personal information to Naukri.

3. How we use your data

We use your personal data only for the following purposes:

  1. Deliver the Service — extract, structure, rewrite, format, watermark, and deliver a PDF resume tailored to your inputs.
  2. Personalize output — tailor bullet points to the target job description you provide.
  3. Process payment — generate a ₹49 UPI payment link via our payment gateway, receive the settlement webhook, and unlock the clean copy.
  4. Respond to your support requests — reply to emails at help.resumebharat@gmail.com.
  5. Detect and prevent abuse — rate-limit runaway senders, verify webhook signatures, block spam.
  6. Measure aggregate service quality — count sessions started, PDFs delivered, average generation time, average ATS score. All aggregation uses hashed identifiers.
  7. Comply with legal obligations — retain payment records for tax purposes for the period required under Indian law (currently 8 years under the Income-tax Act, 1961).

We do not:

  • Sell your data.
  • Use your resume content or WhatsApp messages to train generalized AI models.
  • Send you marketing messages you did not consent to.
  • Share your data with recruiters or job platforms without your explicit action.

7. Security

  • All webhooks (Meta, Razorpay, Cashfree) are cryptographically verified using HMAC-SHA256 with constant-time comparison. Unsigned or forged webhooks are rejected.
  • Your phone number is hashed with a secret pepper before persistence.
  • Signed download URLs for your PDF expire in 300 seconds.
  • All external service credentials are stored as environment secrets and never in source code. Our logger explicitly redacts authentication headers, signatures, tokens and passwords.
  • Access to production systems is restricted to the sole proprietor.
  • We conduct a documented security review before each deployment; our public threat-model summary is available at SECURITY.md.

No system can guarantee absolute security. If we become aware of a breach affecting your personal data, we will notify you and the Data Protection Board of India as required by §8(6) of the DPDP Act, 2023.

10. Automated decision-making

Our service uses AI models to rewrite the content you send us into resume-formatted bullets. The AI output is deterministic in structure but the specific phrasing is generated. You retain full control — you may edit, decline, or discard any AI-generated output before your resume is delivered, and the ATS score we compute is a proxy, not a guaranteed outcome of any recruiter's parser.
